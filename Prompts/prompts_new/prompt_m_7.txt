# CRITICAL VOCABULARY RULE

**PRIORITIZE WORDS FROM THE PROVIDED WORDLIST BUT YOU MAY USE NATURAL SINHALA WORDS BEYOND THE WORDLIST TO CREATE REALISTIC, NATURAL SENTENCES.**

**Vocabulary Guidelines:**
- **60-80% of words** in each sentence should come from the wordlist
- **20-40% of words** may be common, natural Sinhala words outside the wordlist
- New words MUST be:
  - Common, everyday Sinhala vocabulary
  - Natural and appropriate for the **SCHOOL CONTEXT**
  - Grammatically correct
  - Age-appropriate for school children (ages 5-18)
- The goal is to create a **natural, diverse word set** that sounds like real school conversations
- **Do not use any punctuation or special characters**
- Avoid forcing wordlist words unnaturally - prioritize sentence naturalness over strict wordlist adherence

# ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS (ALL CONTEXTS)

❌ NO English words
❌ NO emojis
❌ NO mixed languages
❌ NO numbering in output
❌ NO explanations
❌ NO headers in output
❌ NO empty lines between sentences
❌ NO comments
❌ NO duplicate sentences
❌ NO punctuation marks (. , ! ? etc.)
❌ NO non-school contexts (home life, family activities outside school, shopping, etc.)

# OUTPUT FORMAT (ALL CONTEXTS)

- ONE sentence per line
- NO line numbers
- NO blank lines
- Pure sentence output only
- NO punctuation at end of sentences

# GRAMMATICAL REQUIREMENTS (ALL CONTEXTS)

**Case Markers (use these combinations):**
- Possessive: ගේ, ගෙන් (e.g., මගේ, ගුරුවරයාගේ, මිතුරාගේ)
- Dative: ට (e.g., මට, ගුරුවරයාට, පන්තියට)

**Particles to use:**
- සමග (with), ලෙස (as), ක් (a/an), මෙන් (like), විතරයි (only), වගේ (like), වැනි (such as)

**Allowed combinations:**
- Base noun + ට (පොත → පොතට, පන්තිය → පන්තියට)
- Base noun + ගේ (ගුරුවරයා → ගුරුවරයාගේ)
- Pronoun + ගේ (මම → මගේ)

# CONTEXT 1: STUDENT RESPONSES TO TEACHERS (PRESENT TENSE)

## OBJECTIVE
Generate 1000 declarative present tense sentences of students responding to teachers in school.

## SPECIFICATIONS
- **Sentence Type:** Declarative (statements)
- **Tense:** Present tense ONLY
- **Length:** 1-5 words per sentence
- **Speaker:** Students responding to teachers
- **Context:** **SCHOOL CLASSROOM ONLY** - lessons, classwork, school activities
- **Setting:** Inside classroom, during lessons, school premises

## STRICT SCHOOL CONTEXT REQUIREMENTS

**✓ ALLOWED (School-related only):**
- Classroom activities and lessons
- Understanding/not understanding lessons
- School subjects (ගණිතය, විද්‍යාව, සිංහල, etc.)
- School supplies (පොත, පෙන්සල, මේසය, etc.)
- School locations (පන්තිය, පුස්තකාලය, වැසිකිලිය, etc.)
- Teacher-student interactions
- School routines (පාඩම් කරනවා, පරීක්ෂණය, etc.)
- School feelings about lessons

**✗ NOT ALLOWED (Non-school contexts):**
- Home activities (cooking, cleaning at home)
- Family members doing non-school things
- Shopping or markets
- General daily routines outside school
- Food preparation at home
- Household chores

## CONTENT FOCUS

### Response types (ALL SCHOOL-RELATED):

**1. Understanding/Confusion about LESSONS:**
- තේරෙනවා (I understand)
- තේරෙන්නේ නැ (I don't understand)
- මට අමාරුයි (It's difficult for me)
- ගණිතය තේරෙනවා (I understand math)
- විද්‍යාව අමාරුයි (Science is difficult)

**2. Needing SCHOOL ITEMS:**
- මට පෙන්සලක් ඕන (I need a pencil)
- ඔහුට පොත ඕන (Need the book)
- මට මකනයක් ඕන (I need an eraser)

**3. Going to SCHOOL PLACES:**
- මම වැසිකිලියට යනවා  (I'm going to the toilet)
- අපි පුස්තකාලයට යනවා (Going to the library)
- ඇය පිටියට යනවා (Going to the field)

**4. Doing SCHOOL WORK:**
- මම ලියනවා (I'm writing)
- පාඩම් කරනවා (Doing lessons)
- මම කියවනවා (I'm reading)
- වැඩ ඉවරයි (Work is finished)

**5. Feelings about SCHOOL/LESSONS:**
- මට සතුටුයි (I'm happy)
- මම බයයි පරීක්ෂණයට (I'm scared of the exam)
- ලැජ්ජායි (I'm shy)
